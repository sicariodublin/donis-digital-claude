Privacy Policy

Last updated: 7 June 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how FS.Digital ("we", "us", "our") collects and processes personal data when you visit fsteyerdigital.com ("the website") or engage us for web design and development services.

We comply with the EU General Data Protection Regulation (Regulation (EU) 2016/679) ("GDPR") and the Irish Data Protection Act 2018.

Data controller: FS.Digital, Dublin, Ireland.
Contact for data queries: hello@fsteyerdigital.com

1. What we collect

Contact form submissions

When you submit our contact form, we collect:

  • Your name
  • Your email address
  • The business type you select
  • The message you write

Submissions are handled by Formspree, our third-party form-processing service, and are forwarded by email to us. Formspree acts as a data processor on our behalf and is based in the United States; where personal data is transferred there, we rely on Standard Contractual Clauses. See Formspree's privacy policy at formspree.io/legal/privacy-policy.

Direct correspondence

If you email us, call us, or contact us via LinkedIn or GitHub, we will hold whatever personal data is included in that correspondence (typically your name, email address, and the content of your message).

Client project data

If you become a client, we may also collect: billing address, business name, content you supply for your website (text, images, brand assets), and any data required to set up hosting, domains, or third-party services on your behalf.

Server logs & analytics

Our hosting provider (Cloudflare) automatically logs basic technical information (IP address, browser type, page requested, timestamp) for security and abuse prevention. We do not currently run third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking pixels on this website.

2. Cookies and local storage

This website does not set advertising or analytics cookies. The only client-side storage we use is a single localStorage entry to remember your cookie-banner choice (so we don't show it on every visit). You can clear it at any time from your browser's storage settings.

If we add analytics or marketing tools in the future, we will update this policy and ask for your consent first.

3. Lawful basis for processing

  • Contact form & enquiries: legitimate interests (responding to a person who has contacted us) and, where appropriate, consent.
  • Client work: performance of a contract, and where applicable our legitimate interest in delivering and supporting the agreed services.
  • Legal & accounting records: compliance with legal obligations under Irish tax and company law.

4. How long we keep your data

  • Contact-form enquiries that do not become projects: up to 12 months, then deleted.
  • Client project records, invoices, and correspondence: retained for at least 6 years as required by Irish tax law.
  • Marketing or newsletter contacts (if you opt in): kept until you unsubscribe.

5. Who we share data with

We do not sell your personal data. We share it only with service providers strictly necessary to operate our business:

  • Cloudflare — website hosting.
  • Formspree — contact-form submissions.
  • Google — Google Fonts (served from fonts.googleapis.com / fonts.gstatic.com). Google may receive your IP address as part of serving the font files.
  • Email and accounting providers we use to communicate with you and to keep our books.
  • Irish Revenue and other authorities where we are legally required to do so.

6. International transfers

Some of our service providers (e.g. Cloudflare, Formspree, Google) are based outside the EU/EEA. Where personal data is transferred outside the EU/EEA, we rely on EU Standard Contractual Clauses and/or the EU–US Data Privacy Framework adequacy decision, as applicable.

7. Your rights under GDPR

As a data subject in the EU/EEA, you have the right to:

  • Be informed about how your personal data is used (this policy).
  • Access a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Have inaccurate data corrected.
  • Have your data deleted, where the lawful basis allows it.
  • Restrict or object to certain processing.
  • Data portability — receive your data in a structured, commonly used format.
  • Withdraw consent at any time, where consent is the lawful basis.

To exercise any of these rights, email hello@fsteyerdigital.com. We will respond within one calendar month.

8. Complaints

If you are unhappy with how we have handled your personal data, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Irish supervisory authority:

9. Security

We use reputable hosting providers, HTTPS encryption, and strong authentication on all administrative accounts. No system is perfectly secure — but we take reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect your data.

10. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page will always reflect the most recent version.
